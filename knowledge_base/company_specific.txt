COMPANY-SPECIFIC INTERVIEW KNOWLEDGE BASE
==========================================

## GOOGLE INTERVIEW PROCESS

### Interview Rounds
1. Recruiter Phone Screen (30 min) — background, motivation, salary
2. Technical Phone Screen (45-60 min) — coding on Google Docs
3. Onsite (4-5 rounds): 2-3 coding rounds, 1 system design, 1 Googleyness/Leadership
4. Hiring Committee Review
5. Team Matching

### Google Coding Expectations
- Clean, correct, optimal code — always discuss time/space complexity
- Think aloud — interviewers evaluate thought process
- Start with brute force, then optimize
- Test your code with edge cases
- Google values: correctness > optimization > style

### Google Leadership Principles (Googleyness)
- Comfort with ambiguity — can you make decisions with incomplete information?
- Collaboration — how do you work in teams and handle disagreements?
- Impact focus — have you delivered meaningful results?
- Intellectual curiosity — are you always learning?

### Google System Design Focus
- Design for scale (millions of users, petabytes of data)
- Discuss trade-offs explicitly
- Topics: Consistent hashing, distributed caching, MapReduce, Bigtable, Spanner concepts
- Key question areas: Search, Maps, YouTube, Gmail-scale systems

## AMAZON INTERVIEW PROCESS

### Leadership Principles (All interviews)
Amazon evaluates every question against their 16 Leadership Principles:
1. Customer Obsession — start with customer, work backwards
2. Ownership — act on behalf of entire company
3. Invent and Simplify — find new solutions, embrace change
4. Are Right, A Lot — strong judgment, diverse perspectives
5. Learn and Be Curious — never stop learning
6. Hire and Develop the Best — raise performance bar
7. Insist on the Highest Standards — relentlessly high standards
8. Think Big — bold direction, different thinking
9. Bias for Action — speed matters, take calculated risks
10. Frugality — accomplish more with less
11. Earn Trust — listen, candid, self-critical
12. Dive Deep — stay connected to details
13. Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit — challenge respectfully
14. Deliver Results — focus on key inputs, deliver with quality
15. Strive to be Earth's Best Employer
16. Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility

### Amazon STAR Answers
- Prepare 8-10 strong STAR stories covering multiple LPs
- Use data and metrics in every answer
- Emphasize "I" actions, not team
- Show ownership even when things went wrong

### Amazon Bar Raiser
- Independent interviewer who ensures hiring bar consistency
- Focuses on LPs and overall bar
- Has veto power on hire decisions

## MICROSOFT INTERVIEW PROCESS

### Growth Mindset (Satya Nadella's Core Value)
Microsoft values candidates who:
- Embrace challenges as learning opportunities
- Learn from failure and criticism
- Persist through obstacles
- Find inspiration in others' success
- Have curiosity and willingness to develop new skills

### Interview Structure
1. 4-6 rounds: Coding, Design, Behavioral
2. As Appropriate (AA): additional senior interviewer for final decision
3. Focus on collaborative problem-solving
4. Can use any programming language

### Microsoft Coding Style
- They value communication over perfect code
- Discuss multiple approaches before coding
- Focus on scalability and maintainability
- Testing and debugging are evaluated

## META (FACEBOOK) INTERVIEW PROCESS

### Meta Coding Philosophy
- Speed matters — solve problems quickly
- Focus on optimal solutions
- Strong emphasis on data structures and graph problems
- BFS/DFS, Dynamic Programming frequently tested

### Meta Core Values (Evaluated in Behavioral)
- Move fast — rapid iteration over perfection
- Be Bold — take big risks
- Focus on impact — measure success by real impact
- Be open — transparency and information sharing
- Build social value

### Meta System Design
- News Feed design, Social Graph, Messenger-scale
- Focus on distributed systems, eventual consistency
- Content delivery, recommendation systems

## APPLE INTERVIEW PROCESS

### Apple Culture Questions
- Details matter — perfectionism and attention to detail
- How would you make a product 10x better?
- Cross-functional collaboration across hardware/software
- Privacy and security consciousness

### Apple Technical Focus
- iOS/macOS specific roles: Swift, Objective-C, Core Data, UIKit/SwiftUI

## STARTUPS INTERVIEW PROCESS

### Startup Interview Characteristics
- Often informal, conversational
- CEO or founders may interview directly
- Strong emphasis on culture fit and adaptability
- Take-home projects common
- Questions about handling ambiguity and multiple responsibilities
- Wearing many hats expectation
- Equity and compensation conversation earlier

### Key Startup Questions
- "Where do you see this company in 5 years?"
- "How do you handle changing priorities mid-sprint?"
- "Describe a time you built something from scratch."
- "What would you do in the first 30/60/90 days?"

## FAANG/BIG TECH GENERAL TIPS

### Preparation Timeline
- 8-12 weeks dedicated prep for FAANG
- Week 1-3: Core data structures and algorithms review
- Week 4-6: LeetCode Medium problems (target 100+ problems)
- Week 7-8: System design fundamentals
- Week 9-10: Behavioral story preparation
- Week 11-12: Mock interviews and company-specific prep

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Jumping to code without clarifying requirements
- Not analyzing time/space complexity
- Ignoring edge cases (empty input, null, overflow)
- Not communicating while thinking
- Writing unrunnable code
- Not testing your solution after writing

### LeetCode Patterns to Master
- Two Pointers
- Sliding Window
- Fast and Slow Pointers
- Merge Intervals
- Cyclic Sort
- In-place Reversal of LinkedList
- Tree BFS/DFS
- Two Heaps
- Subsets/Backtracking
- Dynamic Programming patterns
- Graph Traversal
- Topological Sort
